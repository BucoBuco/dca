DC Ambiance are five accomplished musicians arranging and performing in the Django Reinhardt tradition. Their sound is described as a gentle and sophisticated approach to gypsy jazz. We are bass, 2 guitars, clarinet and concertina.

Bill Parmentier

Guitar

Bill grew up playing rock guitar but was bit by the "Django bug" while living on Whidbey Island, WA (home of DjangoFest Northwest) in the early 2000s and befriending the late Dudley Hill of Pearl Django. During this time, he formed and performed with the Harbor Hot Club before moving to Northern Europe. Bill studied with famed gypsy jazz guitarists Robin Nolan and Lollo Meier. Bill extended his love of the genre by attending and jamming at some of the major gypsy jazz festivals in France and the Benelux region.

Cyndy Elliott

Acoustic Upright Bass

Cyndy is a life-long resident of the Washington DC area and currently works as a freelance bassist, performing many different styles of music in big bands, small combos, orchestras, and chamber ensembles, and is the co-leader of the jazz octet Swingtopia. She has performed at many of the major venues in DC, including the Atlas Theatre, Bethesda Blues and Jazz, Blues Alley, Bohemian Caverns, Twins Jazz, and the Kennedy Center. Cyndy has traveled and performed with shows and in concert at various musical venues throughout the United States, Asia, and Europe.

Davorin (Buco) Cavar

Guitar

Davorin Buco (sounds like Bootso, his childhood nickname) Cavar, a native of Sarajevo, began his career as lead with various rock bands here and in Chicago. He turned his attention to the acoustic guitar to focus on his new found love of Gypsy Jazz. Buco has studied with such jazz guitar greats as Tony do Rosario and Alfonso Ponticelli all the while performing in various festivals and clubs between DC and Chicago.

Suzanne Gekker

Clarinet

Suzanne is an active performing classical musician and teacher. She received her music degrees from Yale School of Music and Cincinnati Conservatory. In the DC area, she teaches at the Washington Conservatory of Music and performs with the Washington Concert Opera, National Philharmonic, Alexandria Symphony and is sought after to perform in several local free-lance gypsy jazz groups.

Randy Stein

English Concertina

Randy Stein is a classically trained musician who studied the English Concertina with the famed Matusewitch Brothers, Boris and Sergei. A former circus acrobat and founding member of the Big Apple Circus, Randy performed solo, with various groups and shows and orchestras throughout the USA and Canada in festivals, shows, and theaters including recitals at Lincoln Center and Carnegie Hall. He has recorded with various artists including John Lennon and Yoko Ono on their album Double Fantasy. Randy previously performed with the jazz group The Rodger French Trio. In addition to performing an extensive solo repertoire of Classic American and European Standards, he is currently performing and recording with the Jewish Folk Band, Ein Lanu Z'man and the acoustic folk concertina/melodeon trio The Squeezers.
